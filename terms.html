2. What Antares is

Antares is a browser extension that scans Solana token contract addresses on supported trading platforms and returns a risk verdict (SAFE / CAUTION / DANGER / RUG), a 1000-point composite score, a list of detected flags, and contextual data (top-holder distribution, insider clusters, recent on-chain activity, AI-generated synthesis).

Antares aggregates data from public APIs (DexScreener, RugCheck, GoPlus, Helius, Solscan) and applies its own scoring engine. It does not custody assets, does not execute trades, and does not give financial advice.

5. Verdict accuracy & limitations

Antares makes a best-effort assessment based on the data available at scan time. It can produce false positives (a clean token flagged as risky) and false negatives (a fraudulent token not flagged). Specifically:

  • Upstream APIs (DexScreener, RugCheck, GoPlus, Helius, Solscan) can be incomplete, delayed, or wrong; Antares passes through their data with cross-validation but cannot verify it independently in all cases.
  • The Wash Volume score and Sniper Map retention are derived from heuristic signals (average trade size relative to liquidity, top-10 holder concentration). They are indicative, not forecasts; both can produce false positives and false negatives on tokens with unusual trading shapes.
  • The AI Verdict synthesis is generated by a large language model (Google Gemini). It can hallucinate or misinterpret the underlying data. Use it as plain-English context, not as authoritative analysis.
  • Insider Watch classification relies on transfers visible to upstream indexers. Off-chain wallet movements, secondary accounts, or recently-bridged supply may not be reflected.
